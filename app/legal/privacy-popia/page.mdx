*Effective Date: 26 May 2026*

The Invariance Group is committed to the protection of personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), Act No. 4 of 2013, of South Africa. This document details our processing principles and the security matrices deployed across our operations.

## 2. Core Processing Principles

We process data in strict accordance with the 8 Conditions for Lawful Processing outlined in Chapter 3 of POPIA:

1.  **Accountability**: We take corporate responsibility for all data handled by our local cognitive runtimes.
2.  **Processing Limitation**: Personal information is processed only if necessary and with explicit context.
3.  **Purpose Specification**: Data ingested by our engines is triaged for specific, defined purposes (such as contract compliance audits or exchange control filing validation).
4.  **Further Processing Limitation**: We do not reuse client data for secondary model training or commercial profiling.
5.  **Information Quality**: The client remains the custodian of data accuracy, with automated checks to verify metadata integrity.
6.  **Openness**: Clear notifications are provided to client security committees regarding container routing paths.
7.  **Security Safeguards**: Deployed containers operate inside air-gapped Virtual Private Clouds (VPCs) with zero public ingress.
8.  **Data Subject Participation**: Automated protocols allow rapid retrieval or erasure of customer-identifying records.
