# Human-in-the-Loop Validation Safeguards for Legal AI Pipelines

*Published by the Legal Advisory Board of The Invariance Group*  
*Classification: Technical Briefing • AI Legal Governance*

Delegating contract review and legal triage to artificial intelligence introduces significant regulatory and liability hazards. If a cognitive model incorrectly classifies an indemnity clause or overlooks a POPIA exposure, the parent enterprise faces heavy legal exposure.

This briefing paper details the human-in-the-loop (HITL) safeguards engineered into the **Compliance Invariance (Clarity)** contract triage pipeline.

## 1. Threshold-Based Escalation Boundaries

Clarity evaluates incoming legal documents using semantic score thresholds:

```
[Contract Upload] -> [Analyze Indemnity/Liability Clauses]
                           |
                           v
            Is Confidence Score > 95%?
                  /         \
              (Yes)         (No)
                /             \
  [Automatic Approval]     [Escalate to Internal Legal Counsel]
```

### Classification Risk Categories

1.  **Low Risk (Automated)**: Routine NDA templates matching pre-approved structural layouts with &gt; 95% similarity.
2.  **Medium Risk (Flagged)**: Procurement contracts with modified liability caps or custom data processing addendums. Escalated to internal legal hubs.
3.  **High Risk (Blocked)**: Cross-border service agreements with foreign arbitration clauses or non-standard indemnity commitments. Blocked for attorney redlining.

## 2. Audit Trail & Verification Log

Every automated triage event generates a validation block:
*   **Hash Anchoring**: Contract drafts are mapped to a secure cryptographically signed SHA-256 hash to prevent post-analysis tampering.
*   **Rationale Compilation**: The AI model logs the exact logic path used to classify each clause, ensuring attorneys can quickly audit decisions.
